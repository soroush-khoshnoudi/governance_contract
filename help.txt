1. **Understanding Governance in Smart Contracts:**
   - **Governance modules** are a set of smart contracts that enable a community to influence an on-chain governance system.
   - Key questions to consider when auditing a governance module include:
     - Who participates in decision-making?
     - How do they participate?
     - What actions can they take?
     - Which components of the system can be impacted by governance decisions?
     - Are these decisions binding?
     - Do they take place immediately?
     - How are governance powers distributed among participants?
     - Are there privileged accounts that can overrule governance decisions?
     - What off-chain processes should participants follow?‚Å∏

2. **On-Chain Governance Mechanisms:**
   - **Proposing Changes:** An admin (usually a designated role) of the governance contract can call the `propose` function.
   - **Parameters for Proposing:**
     - `target`: Addresses of the smart contracts or components affected by the proposal.
     - `value`: Amount of cryptocurrency (if applicable).
     - `data`: Additional data related to the proposal.
     - `description`: A brief description of the proposal.
   - **Voting:** Stakeholders (participants) in the DAO can cast their votes using the `castVote` function.
   - **Majority Decision:** The majority vote determines whether the proposal is approved or rejected.‚Åπ

3. **Security Considerations:**
   - Security is paramount for governance contracts to prevent governance powers from falling into the wrong hands.
   - Projects are still figuring out how to implement on-chain governance, and practices are evolving.
   - Follow best practices to mitigate security risks and learn from past mistakes.‚Å∏
